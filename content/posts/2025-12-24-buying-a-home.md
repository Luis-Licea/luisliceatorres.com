Unless you are paying cash for your house, get a conditional mortgage approval before looking at homes for sale. Getting a conditional mortgage approval does not cost you money or lock you into a contract. A mortgage pre-approval just shows how much money a lender like Chase or Zillow is willing to lend you for buying a home. House sellers will give preferential treatment to sellers with conditional mortgage approvals because they are fully prepared to make an offer.

I found a house I liked and got a conditional mortgage approval in the same day, but the house was already sold by the next day. It was only in the market for two days. The importance of having a conditional mortgage approval before looking for houses cannot be understated. Without it, you will miss big opportunities. I found this property and told my real estate agent about it. I should not have done that. He said in passing, unknowing that I would carefully remember, he was in daily contact with people (it sounded like his real estate buddies) who were actively looking to buy properties. I have the slight suspicion that my real estate agent sold the house to his buddies before me because he was quiet the entire day whenever I talked about the property and only replied the next day in the morning when the house was already sold.

Also, I am not completely sure you can trust your real estate agent. If you tell them a really good property you found, they may recommend it to their best clients.

A mortgage pre-approval does not require providing financial evidence. The bank is just trusting your word at this point, but the verification will happen later. For now you can just list the money accounts that you plan to use to pay for the down payment and closing fees without evidence. This can include:

- Bank saving or checking account
- Robinhood, Fidelity, etc investment accounts
- 401K, Roth 401k, IRA, Roth IRA, etc accounts

You do not need to provide info for all your accounts. You only need to show you have enough money for the down payment and closing costs. For example, there may not be any need to list your 401K account if you plan to only use funds from your bank's saving account. You may not even need to list your Fidelity investment account unless you will need to liquify some of your investments.

The lender, usually a bank, basically trusts you not to lie about your finances when getting a mortgage pre-approval. The next step is to get a conditional mortgage approval. This is almost the same as a mortgage pre-approval, but requires providing financial evidence that proves you are not lying about the money in your accounts. The evidence you will have to provide includes:

- W2 Tax forms (showing your yearly income)
- Robinhood statements (showing the amount and type of investments you have)
- Retirement statements (showing the amount and type of investments you have)

For example, if you need 50K for the closing costs but your savings account only has 10K, you must show the bank that your investment account has the remaining 40K. If your investment account has less than 40K, then you must add your 401K, etc. However, I do not suggest emptying your investment or 401K accounts to pay for a down payment. If you need to do so, it is possible to borrow money from your 401K account without penalty.

I suggest you talk over the phone with someone when getting a conditional mortgage approval. They will load your brain with juicy information, details, and opportunities.

Also, always get at least two conditional mortgage approvals from two different lenders so they are forced to compete, price match, or give you a better interest rate.

Also note that banks may not want to lend you money if you are buying certain types of condominiums or dilapidated houses. Those properties are not warrantable, meaning you will not be able to get a conventional or FHA loan. However, you may still be able to apply for regular loan, usually with higher interest rates.

Two important things to consider when getting a loan is whether the loan has a pre-pay penalty or early account closure penalty. Chase loans do not have either of these, so I could theoretically pay a 30 year mortgage in 1 year.

The second thing to consider is whether you will put down a 20% down payment. If not, you will have to pay Private Mortgage Interest (PMI) until you have paid 20% of the house.
